Introduction to the Pathway Allocation Tool

This tool is designed to help clincians determine the most likely outcome for their patients based on a patient's treatment history, demographics and other relevant factors.

This tool is based on a national cohorts of cancer patients, diagnosed in 2004 with their progress tracked for up to 7 years.

Please begin by answering the first question below. The order and type of questions will change based on your answers.

Routes from Diagnosis: Pathway Allocation Tool

The Pathway Allocation Tool is based on the 'Routes from Diagnosis' program, designed to quantitatively describe the survivorship of cancer patients.

Analysis was performed on patients diagnosed in 2004 (2003 and 2004 for brain/CNS patients) and tracked for up to 7 years. The data collected on these patients was then used to segment the population based on their outcomes and survival lengths in to 20-24 different pathways.

This tool uses CHAID to determine decision trees for each cancer based on data collected about patients around diagnosis and during their treatment. The CHAID analysis was performed at 0.1% significance level for each cancer.

For further detail on the tool, please contact Julie Flynn at Macmillan Cancer Support.
